The Season in Brief
Thirteen wins from seventeen. Two 300-plus scores. A semi-final. Gus skippered the side through a campaign they lost just three times — twice to FMPCC Pirates and once to WSCC in what looked suspiciously like a mid-season wobble. Both times they came back and won the next game. That's a captain's side.
Gus: Captain
Gus led from the front — 287 runs at 31.89, two half-centuries, and a composed 82 to set the tone in Round 1. When he wasn't available, Purse kept everything moving. Gus closed out Round 15 with 65 in a 10-wicket demolition. The ICC Rankings (In-house Castley Cricket) have him 4th in his own household — behind Evie, Gibbo, and the dog — but on the field he was firmly number one.
The Road to the Semi Final
Five straight wins to open the season, then two tight losses in Rounds 9 and 10 that stung. Four more wins on the bounce heading into finals. The semi against Swan Athletic — a team they'd beaten by 63 runs earlier — was one of those days where nothing stuck. Mono top-scored with 37 and Jonno took 3-23, but 101 all out was never going to be enough. Swan ran it down by 64. 101 all out is not a semi-final score. These things happen.
About That Grand Final
The week of the Grand Final, the forecast was grim and Jubilee Reserve was waterlogged. Run-ups too wet to play. No result. The group's response was immediate: BC was getting shirts printed. Purse had them down as morally unbeatable as a group. Mono called it undefeated premiers. The page has moved on and so have we.
The Elephant in the Room
Carmo played every game. All 17. Stood behind the stumps every time. 30 dismissals. No fielding award. Purse summed it up in two words. Jonno wanted to know if pipped was a synonym for robbed. The group was unanimous. We will not be investigating this at this time, as no one is willing to explain it with a straight face.
By The Numbers
A 195-run win. Three centuries. The Aviator's 5-30 — the only five-for in the competition. A 10-wicket win in Round 15. Two 300-plus innings. Mono cracking the top 10 all-time ACC run scorers. Jonno hitting 1000 club runs and 200 club wickets in the same match. Not a bad year's work.

Best & Fairest — Joint Winners
Darren Monument
& Jonno Seen
Monument: 480 runs · Avg 60 · SR 107 | Seen: 15 wkts · Econ 3.97 · 9 catches
The numbers made it easy. Mono was the run machine — a century, two fifties, 480 runs at 60, and a strike rate that told you he was never just blocking it. He also cracked the top 10 all-time ACC run scorers during the season. Jonno was the quietly indispensable one: 15 wickets at under 4 an over, 9 catches, and in a single match this season both his 1000th club run and his 200th club wicket — all with munted toes. Two blokes who turned up every week and just did the job.

Bowling Award
Sakun Dissanayake
23 wkts · Avg 20.91 · Best 5-30 · 2 five-fors (March) · 15 matches
The Aviator. Twenty-three wickets — five more than anyone else. The season's only five-for (5-30). Back-to-back five-wicket hauls in March. 612 balls bowled. After every wicket: arms out, wheels down, probably radioing the tower for landing clearance. 'Rare airspace that.' When another team tried to poach him, Purse's response was immediate: 'Ya can't have Sakun! He is the wind beneath our wings.' A deserved winner by any measure.

480 runs · Avg 60.00 · SR 107.14 · 110 not out · 1x 100 · 2x 50

Hall of Fame Inductee
Matt Campbell
14 matches · 339 runs · Avg 26.08 · 14 seasons of service
Cambo earns his spot in the Hall of Fame. 339 runs from 14 matches — never missed a game. Absolutely. He's earned every bit of it.
