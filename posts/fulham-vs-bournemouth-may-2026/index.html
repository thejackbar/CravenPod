§ 06

Four battles that decide it.

Both sides set up in a 4-2-3-1 and press from the front. Across the pitch the individual duels are fine margins, and whichever side wins them collectively will likely win the game.

The teenage record-breaker vs the Dane
Kroupi
Bournemouth · ST
vs
Andersen
Fulham · CB
Kroupi carries twelve Premier League goals into the weekend, equalling the top-flight teenage scoring record. He drops deep to receive, peels wide to stretch the line, and finishes with a composure well beyond his years. Andersen has been Fulham's most composed defender this season, but he was caught for pace at the Emirates. Keeping Kroupi on the back foot from the first whistle is the priority.
The playmaker vs the press-breaker
Wilson
Fulham · RAM
vs
Christie
Bournemouth · CM
With Iwobi absent, Wilson carries the creative weight almost alone. Ten goals this season, several from precisely the half-space pockets Christie will be assigned to close. Christie covers an enormous amount of ground and is aggressive early in his press. Give Wilson a yard and Fulham have a chance. Take it away and Silva's side can look very flat very quickly.
The ten versus the tight-back
Smith Rowe
Fulham · CAM
vs
A. Jimenez
Bournemouth · RB
Smith Rowe starts at number ten, threading between Bournemouth's midfield and defensive lines. Jimenez is quick, but he can be caught when clever movement pulls him inside. If Smith Rowe finds those pockets and turns quickly, he creates the kind of second-phase chances Fulham need. The risk is that Jimenez reads the run early and bodies him out; then Fulham's right side offers little beyond recycling possession.
The overlap vs the tracking run
Robinson
Fulham · LB
vs
Tavernier
Bournemouth · RM
Robinson has been Fulham's most reliable outlet all season, with the engine and technique to get forward from left-back and arrive late in dangerous positions. Tavernier's six goals mean he is not purely a defensive concern; Bournemouth attack down that channel too. Robinson will need to pick his moments carefully. When he goes and it comes off, Fulham's best attacking moves tend to begin there.

§ 08 · The Verdict

Backs to the river, one shot at Europe.

Bournemouth arrive as marginal favourites on the models and carry a fifteen-match unbeaten run to back that up. Fulham are inconsistent, missing Iwobi, and have a head-to-head record in this fixture that gives little comfort. None of that fully accounts for what Craven Cottage is like on an afternoon when something is genuinely at stake. The crowd finds the team. Wilson finds a yard. Three points and next Saturday suddenly looks very different.
