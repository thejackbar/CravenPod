§ 01 · The Brief

The vacancy, the timeline, and the kind of manager the Cottage needs.

Five years was a long time. Now there is a chair to fill. The official Silva announcement at nine o'clock on Tuesday 2 June lifted the lid on a conversation Shahid Khan and Alistair Mackintosh have been having quietly for the better part of a fortnight. The brief: a head coach who can take a squad with a club Premier League points record on its CV and turn the spring-fade pattern into a top-eight push. Possession-based football. Modern recruitment fit. A clear identity. And, this time, a head coach who, by the public reading of the contract impasse with Silva, has more direct control over incoming transfers than the previous one had.

"We will soon appoint a new leader in a timely but deliberate manner, who will meet the standards of our club."

Shahid Khan, official statement, 2 June 2026

The "timely but deliberate" line is the one to hold. It rules out a panic appointment in the next ten days. It also rules out, by implication, a six-week European tour of available coaches. The shortlist below is the one that, by the public reporting cycle of the last seventy-two hours, is the one being worked through. Twelve names. Three buckets. One decision.

§ 02 · Front Runners

The four names the bookmakers and the journalists agree on. One favourite, three close behind.

04
Front runners · Strongest links

Names appearing in multiple reputable outlets, low odds at the major books, and substantive reporting beyond pure speculation.

Kieran McKenna
Favourite · 5/4
Ipswich Town · contracted to 2028

For ·The 2024 LMA Manager of the Year. Two-time Ipswich promotion-winner. Modern possession-based identity that mirrors what Silva built and what the Cottage's squad profile supports. Universally named the favourite across Sky Sports, William Hill (5/4), Betfred (2/1) and Oddschecker (10/3). Sky Sports News reported he is "expected to be high on the list."

Against ·A reported release clause in the £8 million region. Under contract until 2028. Crystal Palace also in the conversation, which complicates the auction. Has never managed in the Premier League before.

Source · Sky Sports · Suffolk News
Thomas Frank
2nd favourite · 4/1
Free agent · ex-Tottenham (sacked Feb 2026)

For ·A seven-year Brentford project that produced Championship play-off promotion, three top-half Premier League finishes and a recognisable style. West London adjacent. Available. Loyalty to a club-building project is exactly the profile that fits a Fulham Tier-2 budget.

Against ·The Spurs spell ended in dismissal after eight months, his first job above mid-table. The reputational gloss has come off slightly. There will be a school of thought that hiring the latest Spurs sacking is the wrong category of appointment.

Source · Bookies.com · William Hill
Oliver Glasner
Contender · 6/1
Crystal Palace · leaving this summer

For ·The strongest recent CV in the available market. An FA Cup and a Conference League trophy with Crystal Palace inside eighteen months. The Austrian's recent press cycle has had him near the top of every "who's next" list at every available club in Europe.

Against ·Sport Witness reports Glasner is holding out for a top-six job. Bayer Leverkusen is reportedly his preference. Fulham is, on the Glasner career trajectory, a step sideways at best. If a bigger club moves first, the conversation is academic.

Source · Sport Witness · Bookies.com
Frank Lampard
Contender · 9/2
Coventry City · just promoted to the PL

For ·The 2025/26 Championship Manager of the Season. Has rebuilt his coaching stock from the Everton sacking onward to an unbeaten run with Coventry in the Championship that ended in promotion. Richard Keys has been consistent that "if Lampard leaves it'll likely be for Fulham."

Against ·Under contract at Coventry. Owes them a Premier League season after the promotion. No top-half Premier League managerial pedigree on the CV. The early Chelsea and Everton spells are easy ammunition for a sceptical Cottage Discord.

Source · Goal.com · William Hill
§ 03 · Safe Bets

The credible profiles. Less hot in the press, more plausible in the room.

02
Safe bets · Strong profile fits

Less prominent in the daily news cycle, but credible profile fits with Cottage history and the squad on the desk.

Liam Rosenior
Outsider · 8/1
Free agent · ex-Strasbourg, ex-Chelsea (sacked April 2026)

For ·Has the sentimental edge. A former Fulham player. Modern, possession-based, identity-driven football. The Strasbourg spell drew praise across European football for the style of play and squad development. The Cottage tends to like its own.

Against ·The Chelsea interim spell ended in dismissal after 106 days in April 2026, which is a fresh dent on a CV that had been building credibility. The "former Fulham player" line cuts both ways: warm reception, but also pressure to do more than the back-room legacy alone justifies.

Source · Yahoo Sports
Pierre Sage
Outsider · 10/1
Lens · contracted

For ·Took Lens to 2nd in Ligue 1 this season. Tactically progressive, modern, the kind of left-of-centre French appointment that has worked well at Premier League clubs in the last five years. Fulham and Crystal Palace are reportedly the two PL clubs monitoring.

Against ·Crystal Palace is reportedly Sage's preference. Under contract at Lens. Unproven outside France. The language barrier and the first-PL-season adjustment are the obvious risk markers.

Source · SportsBoom
§ 04 · Wild Cards

The bookmakers' boards. The four left-field bets with a real case if you squint.

04
Wild cards · Outsiders with a story

Names on the betting boards rather than the back pages. Less probable, but each has a coherent case if you sketch it out.

Kjetil Knutsen
Longshot · 9/1
Bodø/Glimt

For ·European overachiever. Attacking, identity-driven football. The Bodø project has produced famous nights against established European clubs and earned Knutsen a reputation as one of the most distinctive coaches outside the Big Five leagues.

Against ·Has rebuffed previous Premier League approaches. No UK experience. The "outside the Big Five" coaching tier has a high failure rate when it moves to the Premier League cold.

Source · Bookies.com
Edin Terzić
Longshot · 14/1
Free agent · ex-Borussia Dortmund

For ·Took Dortmund to the 2024 Champions League final. Available. A serious tactical mind with the kind of CV that, in a year's time, will be on a shortlist somewhere bigger than Fulham.

Against ·No Premier League experience. Limited public reporting linking him beyond the bookmaker boards. Likely waiting for a Bundesliga return or a top-six European job, not a Tier-2 PL move.

Source · Bookies.com
José Bordalás
Longshot · 12/1
Getafe

For ·Battle-hardened LaLiga survivalist. The Getafe project has been one of the most consistent over-performances against budget in the Spanish top flight for years. A coach who knows how to make less work harder.

Against ·Pragmatic, defensive style clashes with Silva's possession identity and what the Cottage has come to expect on a Saturday afternoon. Named primarily on betting markets rather than in journalist reporting.

Source · Yahoo Sports
Arne Slot
Longshot · 10/1
Free agent · ex-Liverpool (sacked May 2026)

For ·The 2024/25 Premier League champion. Elite pedigree, with a possession philosophy that would fit the Cottage's squad profile in theory. Now available. Yahoo Sports gave him the headline name on the speculation piece.

Against ·This would be a clear step down for Slot's stock, even after the Liverpool dismissal. No journalist has put serious reporting behind the link. Bookmaker noise more than back-page substance. Realistically, Slot waits for a top-five-leagues elite job.

Source · Yahoo Sports
§ 05 · Ruled Out

One name already off the board. The race for Iraola is being run elsewhere.

Andoni Iraola
Out · Liverpool talks
Bournemouth · in talks with Liverpool

Sky Sports reported on 2 June that Liverpool had begun formal talks with Iraola to replace Arne Slot. That effectively removes him from the Fulham conversation. The Basque coach would have been the wishlist appointment for many at the Cottage (the Bournemouth project, the 8th-place finish in 2024/25, the possession identity). It's not happening. We move on.

Source · Sky Sports
§ 06 · The Cravenpod Read
Our shortlist of two
McKenna first. Frank second. Glasner if Bayer don't move.

If the appointment is going to fit the brief Khan laid out this morning, the order on our desk reads McKenna, Frank, Glasner, in that priority. McKenna is the modern possession-based coach who has done two Championship-into-Premier-League cycles. Frank is the proven Premier League project-builder with a west London track record and the most experience of working a tight budget. Glasner is the trophy man whose ceiling is genuinely higher than this job if the Bayer move falls through.

The wild cards are wild cards because that is what they are. Slot will not come for the Cottage. Terzić is the next-tier Dortmund flavour and is waiting for that. Knutsen and Bordalás are bets the bookmakers float to balance the book. The honest read is that one of the top four ends up in the dugout for the 2026/27 opener.
