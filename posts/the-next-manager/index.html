§ 01 · The Brief

The vacancy, the timeline, and how the race has moved since Silva's exit.

Five years was a long time. There is still a chair to fill. The official Silva announcement on Tuesday 2 June lifted the lid on a conversation Shahid Khan and Alistair Mackintosh had been having quietly for the better part of a fortnight. The brief: a head coach who can take a squad with a club Premier League points record on its CV and turn the spring-fade pattern into a top-eight push. Possession-based football. Modern recruitment fit. A clear identity. And, this time, a head coach who, by the public reading of the contract impasse with Silva, has more direct control over incoming transfers than the previous one had.

"We will soon appoint a new leader in a timely but deliberate manner, who will meet the standards of our club."

Shahid Khan, official statement, 2 June 2026

"Timely but deliberate" has, in the days since, looked closer to deliberate than timely. The race has moved underneath the original shortlist. Andoni Iraola signed for Liverpool. Oliver Glasner went into advanced talks with Bayer Leverkusen. Frank Lampard's link, on the latest reporting, has gone cold ("I'm Coventry manager," he told reporters, "I'm enjoying our moment"). Arne Slot, sacked by Liverpool in May, has been added to the Fulham shortlist on TeamTalk's reporting. The names below are the names as they currently stand. Two front runners. Three credible alternatives. Three wild cards on the bookmaker boards. Four already off the board. The numbers below are decimal odds, where 2.50 is the favourite. They will keep moving.

§ 02 · Front Runners

The two names the bookmakers and the journalists agree on. A clear favourite and a clear second.

02
Front runners · Strongest links

Both at the front of the betting and both with substantive journalist reporting linking them to the job. Both also have visible obstacles.

Kieran McKenna
Favourite · 2.50
Ipswich Town · contracted to 2028

For ·The 2024 LMA Manager of the Year. Two-time Ipswich promotion-winner. Modern possession-based identity that mirrors what Silva built and what the Cottage's squad profile supports. Universally named the favourite across the major books at 2.50 (BetVictor 2/1, generally 6/4 elsewhere). Reported by talkSPORT and Football365 as Fulham's "preferred candidate."

Against ·A reported release clause in the £8 million region. Under contract until 2028. Football Insider reports McKenna is leaning toward staying at Ipswich and is expected to reject both Fulham and Crystal Palace (the latter, helpfully for Fulham, has now reportedly turned to Pierre Sage). Has never managed in the Premier League before.

Source · Sky Sports · Football Insider · Football FanCast
Thomas Frank
2nd favourite · 4.00
Free agent · ex-Tottenham (sacked Feb 2026)

For ·A seven-year Brentford project that produced Championship play-off promotion, three top-half Premier League finishes and a recognisable style. West London adjacent. Available. Loyalty to a club-building project is exactly the profile that fits a Fulham Tier-2 budget.

Against ·The Spurs spell ended in dismissal after eight months, his first job above mid-table. The reputational gloss has come off slightly. Crystal Palace is currently doing more concrete work on Frank than Fulham, according to Goal and Football Insider. Currently doing BBC World Cup punditry.

Source · Goal.com · Football365
§ 03 · Safe Bets

The credible profiles behind the two favourites. Three names with real reporting and real cases.

03
Safe bets · Real journalist links, longer odds

Less prominent in the daily news cycle than McKenna and Frank, but credible profile fits with Cottage history and the squad on the desk. Each has been linked by at least one reputable outlet.

Arne Slot
Contender · 11.00
Free agent · ex-Liverpool (sacked May 2026)

For ·The 2024/25 Premier League title winner. Elite pedigree, with a possession philosophy that would fit the Cottage's squad profile in theory. Now available. TeamTalk reported in early June that Slot is genuinely on Fulham's shortlist, lifting him from bookmaker noise on day one to a credibly named contender.

Against ·This would still be a step down for Slot's stock, even after the Liverpool dismissal. The salary and project expectations are realistically out of Fulham's normal band. If he wants to wait for a top-five-leagues elite job, he can. Whether he wants to is the open question.

Source · TeamTalk
Liam Rosenior
Outsider · 11.00
Free agent · ex-Strasbourg, ex-Chelsea (sacked April 2026)

For ·Has the sentimental edge. A former Fulham player. Modern, possession-based, identity-driven football. The Strasbourg spell drew praise across European football for the style of play and squad development. The Cottage tends to like its own.

Against ·The Chelsea interim spell ended in dismissal after 106 days in April 2026, which is a fresh dent on a CV that had been building credibility. The "former Fulham player" line cuts both ways: warm reception, but also pressure to do more than the back-room legacy alone justifies.

Source · Football365
Pierre Sage
Outsider · 12.00
Lens · contracted

For ·Took Lens to 2nd in Ligue 1 this season. Tactically progressive, modern, the kind of left-of-centre French appointment that has worked well at Premier League clubs in the last five years. Fulham was originally one of two PL clubs monitoring.

Against ·Crystal Palace has moved to the front of the queue for Sage on the latest reporting from Football FanCast. He remains under contract at Lens. Unproven outside France. If Palace go for him, the Fulham link is effectively closed.

Source · SportsBoom · Football FanCast
§ 04 · Wild Cards

The bookmaker boards. The three left-field bets, mostly there for the punter rather than the back pages.

03
Wild cards · Boards over back pages

Names listed by the books, with limited journalist reporting backing them up. The case for each is real; the probability is low.

José Bordalás
Longshot · 10.00
Getafe

For ·Battle-hardened LaLiga survivalist. The Getafe project has been one of the most consistent over-performances against budget in the Spanish top flight for years. A coach who knows how to make less work harder.

Against ·Pragmatic, defensive style clashes with Silva's possession identity and what the Cottage has come to expect on a Saturday afternoon. Named primarily on betting markets rather than in journalist reporting. No reputable outlet has reported a substantive Fulham link.

Source · Yahoo Sports (odds board)
Edin Terzić
Longshot · 11.00
Free agent · ex-Borussia Dortmund

For ·Took Dortmund to the 2024 Champions League final. Available. A serious tactical mind with the kind of CV that, in a year's time, will be on a shortlist somewhere bigger than Fulham.

Against ·No Premier League experience. Limited public reporting linking him beyond the bookmaker boards. Likely waiting for a Bundesliga return or a top-six European job, not a Tier-2 PL move.

Source · Bookies.com (odds board)
Kjetil Knutsen
Longshot · 13.00
Bodø/Glimt

For ·European overachiever. Attacking, identity-driven football. The Bodø project has produced famous nights against established European clubs and earned Knutsen a reputation as one of the most distinctive coaches outside the Big Five leagues. Football365 included him in their post-departure Fulham shortlist piece.

Against ·Has rebuffed previous Premier League approaches. No UK experience. The "outside the Big Five" coaching tier has a high failure rate when it moves to the Premier League cold.

Source · Football365
§ 05 · Off the Board

Four names already gone. The race for Iraola and Glasner is being run elsewhere. Lampard's interest has cooled.

Andoni Iraola
Out · Liverpool confirmed
Now Liverpool head coach

Confirmed. Liverpool announced a verbal agreement and a two-year deal in early June. This Is Anfield reported the contract signed and the unveiling underway. The Basque coach would have been the wishlist appointment for many at the Cottage (the Bournemouth project, the 8th-place finish in 2024/25, the possession identity). It went to Anfield. We move on.

Source · Liverpool FC · This Is Anfield · ESPN
Oliver Glasner
Out · Bayer Leverkusen advanced
Leaving Crystal Palace · Leverkusen-bound

The strongest recent CV in the available market is heading for the BayArena rather than SW6. OneFootball, Get Football News Germany and Tribuna all reported in late May / early June that Glasner is in advanced talks with Bayer Leverkusen, with a formal meeting scheduled. The Cottage was never going to get him with a top-six German job on offer.

Source · OneFootball · Get Football News Germany · Tribuna
Frank Lampard
Out · and good
Coventry City · please stay there

The Cottage exhales. Frank Lampard was never the appointment that any honest Fulham supporter was going to spend the summer warming to. The Chelsea-legend résumé is its own conversation in west London, and not one most of us were ever willing to have. The managerial CV (the Derby play-off final defeat, the Chelsea mid-season sacking, the failed Everton interim spell, the Coventry promotion at the end of it) is the kind of work that gets a coach into front-runner odds at a Tier-2 Premier League club and absolutely nowhere near the genuine trust of the Hammersmith End. The notion that a Stamford Bridge favourite son was the answer to a Cottage in transition was always a journalist's hypothetical rather than a fan's preference. The bookies had him at 8.00 the morning of the Silva announcement. The Cottage had him at "absolutely not."

The good news, mercifully, is that he has taken himself out of his own accord. "I'm Coventry manager," he told reporters. "I'm enjoying our moment of being promoted and winning the title." Coventry Live's headline read "Frank Lampard interest cools." Stay there, Frank. Enjoy the Premier League with the Sky Blues. The Cottage moves on with one less name on the list to argue about, and one less name we were going to argue about in the first place.

Source · Coventry Live · Goal.com
Roberto De Zerbi
Out · already at Tottenham
Tottenham Hotspur · since March 2026

Filed under "never quite a Fulham candidate in this cycle, but still appears on betting longshot boards." De Zerbi has been the Tottenham head coach since 31 March 2026. He is in a job. He is not coming to SW6 in the summer of 2026. Mentioned only to settle the Discord question.

Source · Tottenham 2025-26 season
§ 06 · The Cravenpod Read
Our updated shortlist
McKenna first. Frank second. Slot's name on the second page.

The race has tightened. Iraola is gone, Glasner is gone, Lampard has done us a favour and ruled himself out. The two names with both the betting and the journalist reporting on their side are McKenna and Frank, in that order. McKenna is the modern possession-based coach who has done two Championship-into-Premier-League cycles; the question is whether Ipswich (and the player) let him out. Frank is the proven Premier League project-builder with a west London track record and the most experience of working a tight budget; the question is whether Crystal Palace move first.

The third name on our desk is Slot. The TeamTalk reporting matters: this is no longer the bookmaker-noise category. A coach with a Premier League title on the CV being on a serious Tier-2 shortlist is the kind of opportunity that does not come around often. The salary maths is the obvious complication.

If McKenna stays and Frank lands at Palace, the conversation moves to Rosenior (the sentimental former-player edge), Sage (if Palace go elsewhere) and Slot (if he is willing). The wild cards stay wild cards. We will revisit as the news lands. The Cottage's next chapter is starting. Sit tight.
