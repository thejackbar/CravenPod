Fulham strengths

Wilson & Jiménez: the threat Arsenal can't ignore

Harry Wilson's 10-goal, 6-assist season has been one of the quiet stories of 2025-26. Paired with the relentless movement of Jiménez, Fulham have scored from both wings and through the middle. Marco Silva's front two have accounted for 43% of Fulham's league goals this season. Leno is in form, Cairney and Berge give structure in midfield, and back-to-back clean sheets have silenced questions about fragility at the back.

Key Battles

Where Saturday is won

Four duels that will shape the contest. Fulham's path to a result runs through Wilson stretching Hincapié, Andersen out-thinking Gyökeres, and Berge winning the second balls Arsenal feed on. Get any one of those wrong and the Emirates becomes very, very long.

Battle 1 · The Wide Threat
N. Madueke
RW · Arsenal
vs
A. Robinson
LB · Fulham

Madueke's £52m summer move from Chelsea was greeted with murals, vandals and 5,000-name petitions — and he's responded with one of Arsenal's most direct wide threats. Robinson is a model of consistency at left-back, but Madueke's first-step acceleration in 1v1s is among the league's best. The American USMNT captain will need help from Smith Rowe tracking back. If Robinson is left isolated, expect repeated waves of crosses to Gyökeres at the back stick.

Battle 2 · The Striker Test
V. Gyökeres
ST · Arsenal
vs
J. Andersen
CB · Fulham

12 league goals and counting for the Swede, who specialises in the channels Andersen most wants to defend. The Dane is the best aerial centre-back in this fixture and a tone-setter for Fulham's back four — but his recovery pace is a talking point, and Gyökeres lives in behind. Andersen needs to stop the service before it lands. Hold the line too high and Arsenal's midfield runners pick him off; sit too deep and Ødegaard finds half-spaces all afternoon.

Battle 3 · Fulham's Engine Room
H. Wilson
RW · Fulham
vs
P. Hincapié
LB · Arsenal

Fulham's top scorer (10) and top assister (6) versus Arsenal's loan signing from Bayer Leverkusen. Wilson's set-piece delivery and underlapping runs from the right are the single most reliable route to a goal Marco Silva owns. Hincapié is one of the world's best young defenders against a back-foot winger — but he's been an inverted left-back for much of this season, and getting back outside to track Wilson is precisely the sort of duel he was bought to win. The 1v1 of the day.

Battle 4 · The Midfield Screen
Rice + Zubimendi
DM · Arsenal
vs
Berge + Lukic
DM · Fulham

Arsenal's £200m+ midfield axis against Fulham's £40m one. Zubimendi has been the season's most underrated signing — turning Arsenal's possession into chances at a different rhythm. Berge wins more aerial duels than any midfielder in the country and Lukic is the connector who keeps Fulham compact when they don't have it. If Berge can make the second balls a Fulham coin-flip rather than an Arsenal certainty, Silva's side stay in the game well into the final 20 minutes.

The CravenPod verdict

Arsenal too strong, Fulham too short on bodies — but a goal in this one would matter.
Set-piece nightmare looms either way.

Arsenal won the reverse fixture 1–0 with five players who won't start on Saturday. They have never lost a home league match against Fulham. With Eze fresh from his Atlético cameo and Gyökeres on 12 league goals, the title race demands a result and the Emirates demands a performance. Marco Silva will set Fulham up to frustrate, defend the box ferociously, and back Wilson and Jiménez to nick something — exactly as they did at the Emirates in December 2024. We just don't think they hold out for 90.
